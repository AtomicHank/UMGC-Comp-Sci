3. Uniqueness of the minimum spanning trees

Graph from Problem 1:

The minimum spanning tree is unique.

MST:

FI, IE, EB, BH, HC, CD, DA, AG

Reason:

For every edge not in the tree, its weight is greater than the maximum-weight edge on the unique path between its endpoints in the tree. Therefore no non-tree edge can replace a tree edge without increasing the total weight, so there is only one MST.

Graph from Problem 2:

The minimum spanning tree is not unique.

One MST is:

DM, GH, JM, EJ, HM, DF, KL, AD, BC, CM, JK, IM

Another MST is:

DM, GH, JM, EJ, HM, FM, KL, AD, BC, CM, JK, IM

Reason:

The non-tree edge FM has weight 3. In the MST, the path from F to M is F-D-M, whose maximum edge weight is also 3 because DF=3 and DM=1. Therefore FM can replace DF without changing the total weight. Since there is at least one different spanning tree with the same total weight, the MST is not unique.
