3. Uniqueness of the minimum spanning trees

Prim graph:

The MST is unique.

One MST:

FI, IE, EB, BH, HC, CD, DA, AG

Why it is unique:

At each stage there is a uniquely lightest edge crossing an appropriate cut. Even though some weights repeat, no equal-weight alternative can replace one of the chosen edges without increasing the total weight or disconnecting the graph.

Kruskal graph:

The MST is also unique for the connected part of the graph.

One MST:

DM, GH, JM, EJ, HM, DF, KL, AD, BC, CM, JK

Why it is unique:

Although several weights repeat, the chosen edges are forced by the cut and cycle properties. For each required connection, the minimum edge that can connect two different components is unique at the moment it is selected.
