Peer Review 2
Recall that peer review is used as a tool to ensure group cohesion and to track project success throughout the session. Peer review should be only based on quality, timeliness and accuracy of student engagement, and effective communication with peers, where students should provide constructive feedback so that they can analyze their performance for reflective learning.  The feedback from peers does not translate directly to a grade, the instructor will gather the feedback and analyze individual and team performance and identify if there are any critical issues that can hinder the successful completion of the project. Based on this analysis, the instructor will be providing a grade for peer review.

Thoughtfully reflect on your engagement with your group, your contributions and willingness to listen to and consider other perspectives. Include your self-evaluation in the final column of the table on the attached Peer-Review Evaluation Template. Then, consider your partners and provide a rating for each category for each partners. 

Lastly, answer the brief questions at the bottom of the page to provide some additional context to your reviews. This evaluation is worth 25 points. The evaluation will also be assessed by your instructor as part of the grading process of all group members.
